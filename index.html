Is Race "Real"?

My name is Camelia, and in class I've done an experiment based on the question "is race real?" This website explains my claim, evidence and reasoning on why I believe my claim is true.

Claim

From a biological perspective, race is not real.

Evidence

To come to this conclusion, first i put the q tip in my cheek and then put it in the tube then closed it i then handed it over to incubate the tube for 10 minutes. After 10 minutes, we removed it from the heat then used DNA extract for PCR. I then labeled another PCR tube with my initials and then added the PCR master mix, primer mix, and my DNA sample while changing the pipette tips in between and made sure it was mixed well enough. The tube was then put in a PCR machine. When the PCR machine light turned green, it was ready. I filled out a survey asking about my results with my spreadsheet, and later found out who me and other classes matched.

Reasoning part 1

Distinct human races do not actually exist. Regardless of how much race matters in society, science and also this experiment both show it is not real. A lot of people grow up from the same place, have very similar features and assume these traits come from being the same race. While it is true that these traits have been passed down from parent to child, it is only because of the fact that the child is their parents' offspring, and not so much because of what we call a race.

Reasoning part 2

The results from this experiment gave me and most other people matches that didn't quite make sense looking at it with the idea of race in mind. If biological race was real, we would have experienced a lot more people from the same or similar countries and communities being matched.

2nd claim

While I don't believe race is real from a biological perspective, i do believe race is socially and psychologically real.

Evidence

The implicit test is a test that measures how fast a person matches a lot of different races with negative or positive words. In the blue eyes and brown eyes class experiment, the students were treated differently based on eye color and the group that was treated better than the other or was superior did better work and had more confidence while the other less superior group struggled to do the same thing the other group was doing.

Reasoning 2 part 1

What it means for something to be socially real is when people or a society believe something is real even if it is not confirmed and it affects how people are seen and treated. On the other hand, what it means for something to be psychologically real is when it affects someone's thoughts and feelings along with plenty of other things.

Reasoning 2 part 2

I know the idea of the social and psychological reality of race is true because race affects how people think and how they are treated. The evidence from the blue eyes and brown eyes experiment shows how people change how they act or their attitude when they are treated differently. Even though eye color doesn't make anyone different, how they were treated changed how they felt and acted. If the opposite claim was true, people would not be affected based on race.
